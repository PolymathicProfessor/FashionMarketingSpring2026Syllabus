Welcome

Fashion marketing is more than selling clothes—it’s selling meaning, identity, culture, and aspiration. This course builds your foundation with textbook concepts while using a Black cultural lens to analyze how trends are created, branded, distributed, and monetized.

Purpose

You will leave this course more culturally competent and career-ready — able to analyze brands, evaluate consumer behavior, and design strategies that are both effective and ethical.

Course objectives

What you will be able to do by the end of the semester.

  • Explain core fashion marketing concepts, theories, and frameworks using professional vocabulary.
  • Analyze fashion marketing through a cultural lens, including identity, power, access, and Black influence.
  • Evaluate fashion consumer behavior (perception, motivation, attitudes, reference groups, and social class).
  • Apply segmentation, targeting, and positioning strategies to fashion brands and markets.
  • Assess ethical, social responsibility, and sustainability issues within fashion production and marketing.
  • Develop career-ready fashion marketing recommendations through applied analysis, projects, and presentations.
